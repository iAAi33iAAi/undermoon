## 14. THE PROOF STRATEGY — DIRECT VS CONTRAPOSITIVE

This is a technical insight that matters for anyone building formal proofs.

**The key discovery:** Two logically equivalent statements can have different
inductive strength in TLA+ proofs. The contrapositive form is always stronger
for gate-based invariants.

```
Direct form (readable):
  EXECUTING ⟹ audit_written = TRUE
  "If executing, then audit was written."
  Problem: requires trace reasoning for ExecuteOperation case

Contrapositive form (inductively strong):
  audit_written = FALSE ⟹ ¬EXECUTING
  "If audit not written, then not executing."
  Advantage: all five action cases are trivial

Why the contrapositive is stronger:
  The action's PRECONDITION (audit_written = TRUE) directly falsifies
  the antecedent of the contrapositive (audit_written = FALSE).
  The implication is vacuously true — no consequent reasoning needed.
```

**The recommended proof structure:**
1. State the theorem in direct form (readable)
2. Prove it via contrapositive form (inductively strong)
3. Connect with a bridge lemma (classical logic — trivial)

**The constitutional significance:**
The contrapositive form is the correct formal encoding of CI-07.
CI-07 says "audit BEFORE execute" — a prevention, not a consequence.
The contrapositive says "¬audit ⟹ ¬execute" — the same prevention.
The gate builds a door that does not exist, not a door that is locked.
